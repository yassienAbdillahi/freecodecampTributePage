No not that one

CR7 vs R9

These days when the name Ronaldo is mentioned most people, especially young people, immediately think of Cristiano, the Portuguese superstar who has been at the center of world football for the last 18 years. But before there was ever a CR7, R9, also known as O Fenômeno, was ripping defences to shreds with his unique combination of pace, power, balance, grace and, above all, technique.

So who is he?

Ronaldo Luís Nazário de Lima, born 18 September 1976, is widely considered one of, if not the, greatest player of all time. José Mourinho, himself one of the most successful managers in the modern era, holds this view. This is him speaking about Ronaldo:

if we are talking strictly about talent and skill, nobody surpasses Ronaldo [Nazario]. When he was at Barcelona with Bobby Robson, I realised that he was the best player I'd ever seen....the talent that that 19-year-old boy had was something incredible."

How did he play?

Lightning fast and ridiculously strong, Ronaldo was blessed with the physical attributes to compete at the highest level of the game. What made him special and once in a generation though was the fact that he was equally skilled and gifted in the technical department. His touch, vision, passing, finishing and legendary dribbling meant that he posessed the elite-level abilities of several different attacking positions; number 10, winger AND striker.

What did Ronaldo win?

Individual Awards

His individual accolades include being named FIFA World Player of the Year three times, and winning two Ballon d'Or awards as well as a host of others that if I listed right now would literally take up the whole page.

Team Titles

In his club career he won a total of 13 tropies while at international level he was arguably even more successful, winning the World Cup and Copa América twice each as well as the FIFA Confederations Cup and a bronze medal at the 1996 Summer Olympics.

The full list of Ronaldo's trophies is below:

  • Cruzeiro: Copa do Brasil (1993) and Campeonato Mineiro (1994)
  • PSV Eindhoven: KNVB Cup (1995/96) and Johan Cruyff Shield (1996)
  • Barcelona: UEFA Cup Winners' Cup (1996/97), Copa del Rey (1996–97) and Supercopa de España (1996)
  • Inter Milan: UEFA Cup (1997/98)
  • Real Madrid: La Liga (2002/03), Supercopa de España (2003) and Intercontinental Cup (2002)
  • Corinthians: Copa do Brasil (2009), Campeonato Paulista (2009)
  • Brazil: FIFA World Cup (1994, 2002), Copa América (1997, 1999), FIFA Confederations Cup (1997), Summer Olympic Games bronze medal(1996)
